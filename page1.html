TRIPTYCH

★ Design a triptych in which the link between the three of you is clear. ★

When we started looking for a connection between us, we found out that all three of us were drummers/used to play the drums. We tried to make this clear through the typography. By placing typical drum sounds several times over each other, we wanted to give the impression of vibration (like a drumhead vibrates when you hit it). 
size: 3x A1

VINYL DEPECHE MODE - SONGS OF FAITH AND DEVOTION

★ Design an LP sleeve for Depeche Mode - Songs of Faith and Devotion.
Try to imagine that the existing cover has been refused as a proposal by the artist or label,
so they asked you to come up with a new concept. ★

I used two old photographs of my grandmother for the front and back. The lilies you see on the front were standing next to the dead body of my grandmother’s mother, who was very religious. Since the record is about faith, I wanted to use an image that represents having faith (here: believing in God) and being devoted to it.
Lilies are flowers you often see on graves and are therefore associated with death and faith. I turned the colours purple to represent mourning. The picture on the back is a photo of 3 men walking home after the funeral.
I zoomed so the emphasis would be on their body language, which clearly suggest they’re mourning.
I used pink for the songtitles to make it less dark. For the inner sleeve I put the lyrics because I wanted it to be like a booklet you get in church with the lyrics of the songs the priest sings. The sticker on the record itself is also an old photo of flowers on a grave.
size: 320 mm x 320 mm

PARIS MATCH

★ Design your own version of the weekly French magazine Paris Match.
The subject has to be something that you associate Paris with.
Use your own imagery taken during the schooltrip to Paris.★

When I think about Paris, I think about romance. For the subject, I was inspired by an ad I saw on a pole of an Italian man looking for a woman to spend his life with. It made me think of people who although they live in the so-called ‘city of love’ are lonely and are looking for someone to love. It’s also about trying to find your match in different ways. Some do it the old-fashioned way by putting up an ad, some do it with the help op social media and the internet. I collected photographs of people sitting alone and tried to match them. I used the colours red and blue to represent two opposites. In the end of the book you see that the two colours are starting to mix into purple, which means they are a perfect match and now are one. 
size: 260 mm x 345 mm

SANDWICH SOCCER

★ Create a graphic identity for your group by designing a sandwichboard for everyone that clarifies your idea. Invent regulations and bring a group-performance of 5 minutes. ★

We are called ‘Hellvetica’ and protest against graphic perfection. We found our inspiration through the protest boards of the Westboro Baptist Church, whose slogans aren’t very tasteful to say the least. We tried to make our posters bad on purpose (yet so bad they become good) because protest signs usually do not show much class.
The fire is our center. It represents a fire pit where we, as protesters, keep going to for warming ourselves up. 
size: 16x A1 (for front and back of 9 sandwichboards)

RONALD MCDONALD GAZETTE

★ Design a 24-page newspaper with the topic Donald Trump and Ronald McDonald. The graphic identity of the newspaper will be determined by a 24-piece dingbat font that you have to design for this assignment. The only fonts you’re allowed to use are Times New Roman and Neue Helvetica.
You also have to use all the given texts.  ★

I designed a Ronald McDonald Gazette where I label everything that Trump says as fake news. I tried to visualise this by putting splashes and rotten tomatoes all over his texts/quotes/tweets. By the end of the newspaper it’s not readable anymore. My dingbat font is 24 times ‘booo’ which refers to literally booing someone. The splashes and the booing is only used in Trump’s texts. The McDonald texts are very clean and show a huge contrast.

FAKE ALTERNATIVE FACTS NEWS 

★ Design a website where you integrate three random subjects chosen by chance. ★

My subjects were: Paul Nipkow, Carl Linnaeus and 2.3 seconds. I tried to recreate the famous nipkowdisks by making my own disks in real life with a lasercutter. I then placed them the botanical garden of Ghent as a reference to Carl Linnaeus who formalised the modern system of naming organisms. The typography is the Latin names of the plants that stand behind the nipkowdisks. When you click on the disk it spins for 2.3 seconds
